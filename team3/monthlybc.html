One Month to Three Months - Birth Control Methods

Method How It Works Advantages Disadvantages
Vaginal Ring A small, flexible ring that is inserted into the vagina and replaced every three weeks. One size fits all, Private, Can make periods more regular and less painful,You can become pregnant right after stopping Can increase vaginal discharge, Does not protect against HIV or other STIs
Patch Applied to the skin for 3 weeks, followed by a patch-free week. Regulates Periods, You can become pregnant right after stopping Can irritate skin under the patch, Does not protect against HIV or other STIs
Shot (Depo-Provera) The shot must be given every 3 months and suppresses ovulation. Private, Usually decreases periods, Helps prevent cancer of the uterus, Can be used while breastfeeding May cause delay in getting pregnant after you stop the shots, Side effects may last up to 6 months after you stop the shots, Does not protect against HIV or other STIs
