IUD Hormonal (Mirena) A small T-shaped device inserted into the uterus, containing a low dose of the hormone levonorgestrel to prevent ovulation. Low hormonal dose, doesn't interrupt intercourse, and can be used by those who can't take estrogen. Possible hormonal side-effects; may cause irregular bleeding; small risk of expulsion of the IUD in the first year; slight increased risk of infection;risk of tubal infertility and/or uterine perforation; increased risk of miscarriage,ectopic pregnancy,and infection if pregnancy occurs.
IUD Copper (Paragard) A small T-shaped device inserted into the uterus, that impairs sperm mobility, thus preventing fertilization. No hormonal side-affects, can be used when breastfeeding and those who can't take estrogen; FDA approved for 16 and older; can be used as emergency contraceptive for 5 days. May cause heavier or irregular bleeding and increased cramping in the first 3 months; small risk of expulsion of the IUD in the first year; slight increased risk of infection; risk of tubal infertility and/or uterine perforation.
